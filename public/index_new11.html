CORONA VIRUS - COVID 19 🦠




Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by a newly discovered coronavirus named as severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus a.k.a (SARS-CoV-2).
The COVID-19 virus spreads primarily through droplets of saliva or discharge from the nose when an infected person coughs or sneezes, so it’s important that you also practice respiratory etiquette (for example, by coughing into a flexed elbow).
At this time, there are no specific vaccines or treatments for COVID-19. However, there are many ongoing clinical trials evaluating potential treatments.


SYMPTOMS

Reported illnesses have ranged from mild symptoms to severe illness and death for confirmed coronavirus disease cases.
These symptoms may appear 2-14 days after exposure (based on the incubation period of MERS-CoV viruses).

  • Fever
  • Cough
  • Shortness of Breath


When to Seek Medical Attention ?

If you develop emergency warning signs for COVID-19 get medical attention immediately. Emergency warning signs include*:

  • Trouble breathing
  • Persistent pain or pressure in the chest
  • New confusion or inability to arouse
  • Bluish lips or face

*This list is not all inclusive. Please consult your medical provider for any other symptoms that are severe or concerning.

COVID-19 Virus Myths Decoded !

From the evidence so far, the COVID-19 virus can be transmitted in ALL AREAS, including areas with hot and humid weather. Regardless of climate, adopt protective measures if you live in, or travel to an area reporting COVID-19. The best way to protect yourself against COVID-19 is by frequently cleaning your hands. By doing this you eliminate viruses that may be on your hands and avoid infection that could occur by then touching your eyes, mouth, and nose.

FAQs



  1. What is the source of your data?

    • For Global Cases: Data is scraped from WHO's Official website, CDC and Wikipedia
    • For Indian Cases: Official Data is scraped from Ministry of Health and Welfare's Official Website
    • For Indian Cases: Unofficial Data is scraped from a CrowdSourced Patient Database driven by a great group of volunteers - COVID19.org - Do visit their site. Its really great!


  2. Well, if you are considering such good sources, then how do you explain the difference in stats for India in the world and country specific analytics?

    That's a great question! So the MFHW website updates the stats on a scheduled basis (instead of what they claim as "live") but WHO and other sources used for global data gets updated pretty rapidly compared to it. Hence, this delay between updation of MFHW and global data websites creates this issue. However, I've added another analytics for India (you can see it by clicking the "Unofficial Source" tab in the same section). This analytics is taken from a crowdsourced database which is updated rapidly (although is not officially considered) but has been proven to be valid most of the times. Stats from these sources may match up well for India in the global stats as well.


  3. Are you planning to add an API for this analytics?

    Yes, I am working on making this a fully functional API. However, there are already some great APIs (by the time this project gets deployed XD) which can be used for both the stats.


  4. Why are you working on this?

    Initially, I started working on it to fillup the gap of such real-time websites and APIs. Another reason, was to document such kind of data, so that people can be aware of the outbreak of the diesease and could realise the gravity of the situation. I also saw many folks getting confused and deluded, either through Tele-news or by whatsapp forwards.This became one of my motivations for adding things besides the stats and geoanalysis like myth busters, precautions, symptoms , disease description and essential links etc.


  5. How do I report a bug?

    Just write down your bug on the Tawk messenger by clicking at the bottom right message icon. (Also, you won't recieve any bounty on reporting a bug. Don't make it a business 🤪)


  6. Why is everything aligned so strangely on my computer? Have you hard-coded the dimensions?
